Help Topics

This section provides information on using the various features and functions of the online AICPA Code of Professional Conduct.

A free password-protected account must be created to use certain features. When you try to use one of these features, if you are not already logged in you will be prompted to input your e-mail address and password. If you forget your password, you may have it e-mailed to you by selecting the "Forgotten Password" prompt.

To enhance the usability of the site, we recommend your screen resolution be set to a minimum of 1024 x 768 and, for optimal usage, we recommend 1280 x 1024. We also suggest you use a current version of your browser. If you are using Internet Explorer 9 or lower, the paragraph numbers will appear slightly above the paragraph content.

Additionally, the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct will timeout after 60 minutes of idle time. Once the system times out, you will need to restart the session to continue working. You will receive an alert message that your session is about to timeout. If no action is taken when you receive this message, your session will automatically end about 10 minutes later. If you click OK, your session will remain active.

Support is available from 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. Eastern time, Monday through Friday.

Phone: 1-888-777-7077

E-mail: service@aicpa.org

Please provide as much information as possible when contacting Customer Service for support, including:

  • the exact error message you received, and specifically what you were doing when you received the error.
  • information on your system configuration, including browser version.

If you have ethics inquiries, please send us an e-mail at ethics@aicpa.org.

Features Back to top »

The online AICPA Code of Professional Conduct contains many unique features that allow you to explore the content in a customized way.

Pop-up Terms

Terms defined in the "Definitions" section [ET 0.400] of the Code of Professional Conduct appear throughout the Code in pop-up windows. To view a pop-up, place your curser over any term that appears in blue italic and the definition pop-up box will appear on your screen. If the definition is longer than the box, a scroll bar will appear on the right margin of the box so you can view the definition in its entirety.

You can opt not to view the pop-up windows by selecting "Disable Definition Popup" under "Tools" at the bottom of your screen. This selection will remain until you either deselect the box or clear the "cookie" history in your browser history.

View Complete

Selecting "View Complete Topic" allows you to view all of the content of a particular topic — such as its rule and interpretations — on one scrollable screen rather than clicking through the pages using the "<PREV" and "NEXT>" buttons, visiting the Table of Contents, or using the breadcrumb dropdown menu.

Likewise, "View Complete Subtopic" allows you to view just the interpretations within a topic on one scrollable screen rather than clicking through a series of pages or using one of the site's other navigational tools.

PDF Download

The Code of Professional Conduct is available in its entirety as a PDF download. You are able to do this by selecting "Download Code of Professional Conduct PDF" from the AICPA Publications Resources list on the homepage,

or selecting "Download PDF of Code" from the bottom of your screen while in any section of the Code.

The following sections discuss the navigation features.

Homepage

The homepage is your first access to the information contained in the online AICPA Code of Professional Conduct. You may return to the homepage at any time by clicking on the home icon on the Breadcrumb (at the top of the site) or the toolbar (at the bottom of the site).

Start by clicking on the "+" button. From there, follow the link for the Code. As you navigate deeper, you will notice that the Breadcrumb at the top of the page expands to include the higher-level pages you've navigated through, such as the Code overview and title page. To find the specific table of contents for the Code, click in the Code's Breadcrumb node to pull down the detail. See more about the Breadcrumb by clicking here.

AICPA Publications Resources

The AICPA Publications Resources list on the right side of your page provides quick access to resources intended to help you find specific content:

  • Table of Contents. This link takes you to the table of contents.
  • Advanced Search. This link will take you to the Advanced Search form.
  • Quick Find. This link will take you to the Quick Find tool.
  • Help. This link will take you to this help page.
  • Download Code of Professional Conduct PDF. This link opens the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct as a downloadable PDF.

    • Breadcrumb

    The Breadcrumb function is located at the top of the site and is the primary navigation feature of the AICPA online Code of Professional Conduct. It serves as both a Breadcrumb and a table of contents. (A separate table of contents is also available. For additional details about the separate table of contents, click here.) The Breadcrumb will display (1) your current location, (2) the preceding documents in the hierarchy, and (3) the location(s) or document(s) that you can go to next.

    As you navigate deeper into the content, a Breadcrumb link is added for each level that you move through. As additional levels are added, the preceding links will compress and only their icons will display. If you mouse over one of these compressed Breadcrumb links, it will expand. If you move the mouse off of the dropdown menu, it will compress again.

    Each individual Breadcrumb link has an orange down arrow . Clicking on this arrow (or anywhere in the Breadcrumb node) will open a dropdown menu that consists of all of the next level documents within the hierarchy.

    The Home button will always appear at the far left of the Breadcrumb. Clicking on the button will take you to the homepage, and clicking on the orange down arrow next to icon will give you a dropdown menu of the main sections on the homepage.

    Toolbar

    The toolbar is at the bottom of the site and consists of a horizontal list of tabs corresponding to sections of the site. Refer to the "Toolbar" section for more information.

    Table of Contents

    The table of contents is available from several areas on your screen, including the "Resources" section of the homepage, the "Tools" tab on the toolbar, and the top left of your screen once in the Code.

    When you first open the table of contents, it shows the top level of the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct. Click the "+" button next to the heading in the table of contents to expand the list and reveal subheadings and the "–" button next to the headings in the table of contents to collapse the list and hide subheadings. You may view a document by clicking the document name in the table of contents.

    To synchronize the table of contents with the current document you have open, click on the "SYNC TOC" button at the top of the table of contents. To return to the document you were previously viewing, select the "Return to Document" button.

    Search

    Search is an excellent way to find the content you need. The online AICPA Code of Professional Conduct includes basic and advanced search options, the ability to refine your searches in several different ways, and a saved search function. Refer to the "Search" section for more information.

    Opening a New Document

    To open a new document, simply click "Documents" tab in the toolbar. Buttons corresponding to documents you already have open will appear along with a "+" button. Click the "+" button. You will then have four options to start a new document (see the image below):

    • Starting at the same location as your current document
    • Performing a new search
    • Using the Quick Find tool
    • Starting at the homepage

    In addition, clicking on any link in the Table of Contents (accessible from the homepage and the toolbar) will open a new document as will clicking the "new document" icon to the right of any link.

    Up to 10 documents may be open at one time. If you attempt to open more than 10 documents, you will be prompted to close some of the documents you already have open.

    Quick Find

    Selecting "Quick Find" from the AICPA Publications Resources listing on the home page will allow you to easily locate guidance in the Code of Professional Conduct. Select the part of the Code that you are interested in viewing (that is, Preface, Part 1, Part 2, Part 3, Appendixes) and then select the topic from the resulting list. You will automatically be taken to the selected topic in that part of the Code of Professional Conduct.

    Clicking that button will bring up a sign in page if you are not already logged in. Once you log in, you can add a bookmark to that section. When you've added a bookmark, you will notice that the button changes from "Add Bookmark" to "Delete Bookmark."

    To access your bookmarks, click the My Bookmarks button in the Tools section of the toolbar.

    Here, you can delete bookmarks or go to the bookmarked section. Click on the link or the new document icon to go to your bookmarked content.

    Both of those options will bring up a preview screen:

    To print the current section you are viewing, click the "Print" button. To create a PDF instead of printing, select the "print PDF" option. The "Print related subpages" option allows you to print not only the document you are viewing, but any related subpages as well.

    E-mail

    Links

    Basic Search

    The basic search box enables you to do single or multiple word searches.

    • Enter the word(s) you want to search for and click the magnifying glass icon.
    • The following will display:
      • A list of sections of the Code and the number of search results in each section. This list may be used to refine your results to a specific subsection of the Code. For further details on this feature click here.
      • The advanced search feature, which enables you to do more precise searching. A list of results for your search term. Click the title of the desired result to view the text of the document.
    • Once in a document, you are easily able to return to your current search results by clicking the "Results" button.
    • To return to the content you were viewing prior to starting your search, click the "Back" button located next to the "Results" button.

    Advanced Search

    The advanced search enables you to perform a focused search by selecting from a list of options. The advanced search can be reached from several places, including the "Advanced Search" button at the top of your document area, the "Resources" section of the homepage and from the "+" sign under the "Documents" tab on the toolbar.

    • Enter the word or phrase that you want to search for.

    • Select whether you want to search for "All Words," "Any Words," "Exact Phrase," or "Boolean Search".
      • All Words - Each word must be present in the document to return a hit result.
      • Any Words - At least one of the words must be present to return a hit result.
      • Exact Phrase - All words must be present in the same order that you have typed them in to return a hit result.
      • Boolean Search - Allows you to refine your queries to focus on more specific or more general information than may be found with a single word or phrase search. Click here for more details on Boolean searching.
    • You have the option to narrow your search to a specific section of the Code. To do so, select the desired "collection" from the dropdown menu.

    • Select whether you want to show excerpts in context of the document in your search results. If you do not want excerpts to display, de-select the box for "Show Excerpts."

    • Click the "Search" button to perform the search.

    • A list of results will be displayed. Click the title of the desired result to view the text of the document.

    Boolean Operator Searches

    The Boolean operators allow you to refine your queries to focus on more specific or more general information than may be found with a single word or phrase search.

    Boolean operators require a word or phrase on either side of the operator. For example, dog or cat is a valid query, but or cat dog is not. The exception is the not operator. The not operator can be used before a term and still be valid. For example, not cat dog returns the same result as dog not cat. There are three Boolean operators that you can use in the AICPA Online Professional Library: and, or, not. See Operator precedence for a discussion of the precedence (order of execution) of these operators and how to override the precedence.

    And
    Use the And operator to search for multiple words in the same document.

    The And operator may be specified in either of two ways: a single space between words or the word and.

    Query

    Explanation

    view codes
    view and codes

    Finds all documents that contain both view and codes

    accountant AND attorney and doctor

    Finds all documents that contain accountant, attorney, and doctor


    Or

    Use the Or operator to search for multiple words in the document collection (the words may or may not be in the same document).

    The Or operator is specified using the word or.

    Query

    Explanation

    view or codes

    Finds all documents that contain either view or codes (may contain both)

    accountant or attorney and doctor

    Finds all documents that contain accountant and either attorney or doctor (or both attorney and doctor) (See Operator precedence for additional explanation on these results)

    Not
    Use the Not operator to search for documents that do not contain a particular term or set of terms. Not is actually a unary operator (it can be used by itself), but it is often used in between two terms (find documents that contain A but not B).

    Generally, the best way to think of the Not operator when used between two terms (A not B) is "find documents with A and find documents without B" (A and not B).

    The Not operator is specified using the word not.

    Query

    Explanation

    view not codes

    Finds all documents that contain view but that do not contain codes

    not account

    Finds all documents that do not contain account

    Operator precedence
    The query operators have a precedence order. That is, when two more operators are used in a query, the results of one operator will be evaluated before another operator. Understanding the precedence -- and how to override the precedence -- can assist you in finding the information you need.

    The operator precedence is: Not, And, Or
    If the same operators are used in a query (for example, if Or appears twice in a query), the operators are evaluated from left-to-right.

    Viewing and Refining Your Search Results

    At the top of the Search Results page, you will see results from each section of the Code specified in your search. Use these links to narrow your results to a specific topic or subtopic. You will be able to narrow your results even further when you drill down. As you drill down, the search results will automatically reflect the narrowing of your results.

    The rest of the page shows your results by section within topics or subtopics. For example, the first result in the search above is "1.000.010 Conceptual Framework for Members in Public Practice." You can click the title to go directly to that section or you can click the "+" icon next to the title to open the "Hit Details" box. This will show the subsections of that section and the number of search results within each subsection, as shown below.

    Navigating Search Results Within Documents

    After clicking into a document that contains your search query, use the gray "Search Hits" box on the top right of the screen to navigate through the search hits. Each hit will be highlighted in the text.

    The "Prev" and "Next" buttons will take you to the previous and next hits within the document you are viewing. Use the ≤ and ≥ buttons in the "Search Hits" box to navigate to the previous and next documents that contain your search term. (The previous and next documents are in the same order they appeared in the search results.)

    Once you've located your desired hit, clicking the "X" in the "Search Hits" box will close the box and keep your current document open.

    Modifying and Saving Your Search Back to top »

    To modify your search, enter your new search words and options in the form above the search results and click the "Update Search" button. To save your search, click the "Save Search" button, then enter the desired title in the pop-up box. When you click "Submit," the current search results will be saved.

    Saved searches are retrievable from the Tools menu of the toolbar. This feature lists all of your saved searches by title and allows you to access the saved search results by clicking on a title. You can also rename or delete any of your saved searches.
