Navigating the SiteBack to top »

There are many ways to navigate the AICPA Online Professional Library:

  • Homepage links
  • The combined Breadcrumb/table of contents that is always at the top of the screen
  • A separate table of contents available from the “Resources” section of the homepage as well as the “Tools” menu of the toolbar
  • Search
  • New document options
  • What’s New feature
  • Quick Find tool
  • Back button
  • Next and previous buttons within a document
  • Links
  • Bookmarks

The following sections discuss the navigation features.

HomepageBack to top »

The homepage is your first access to the information contained in your AICPA Online Professional Library subscription. You may return to the homepage at any time by clicking on the home icon on the Breadcrumb (at the top of the site) or the toolbar (at the bottom of the site).

Some features of the homepage include:

  • Home Titles Section

    This section is your initial entry to the AICPA Online Professional Library. It displays all of the titles that you subscribe to.

    Start by clicking on the “+” button of any of the main sections. From there, follow the links to subsequent levels to access the specific content you are looking for.

    As you navigate deeper, you will notice that the Breadcrumb at the top of the page expands to include the higher-level pages you’ve navigated through. To find the specific table of contents for a given publication, click anywhere in the appropriate Breadcrumb node to pull down the detail. See more about the Breadcrumb by clicking here.

  • AICPA Publications Resources

    This list provides quick access to resources intended to help you find specific content:

    • What's New

      This link takes you to the What's New feature. This feature provides a summary of the latest changes to your AICPA Online Professional Library subscription in an easy-to-access list organized by product.

    • Table of Contents

      This link takes you to the table of contents.

    • Advanced Search

      This link will take you to the Advanced Search form.

    • Quick Find

      This link will take you to the Quick Find tool.

    • FASB ASC Go To

      This link takes you to the Go To function for the FASB Accounting Standards Codification™ content.

    • FASB ASC Cross Reference Tool

      This link takes you to the Cross Reference tool for the FASB Accounting Standards Codification™ content.

    • Help

      This link will take you to this help page.

Breadcrumb Back to top »

The Breadcrumb function is located at the top of the site and is the primary navigation feature of AICPA Online Professional Library. It serves as both a Breadcrumb and a table of contents. (A separate table of contents is also available. For additional details about the separate table of contents, click here.) The Breadcrumb will display (1) your current location or the document you are currently viewing, (2) the preceding documents in the hierarchy, and (3) the location(s) or document(s) that you can go to next.

As you navigate deeper into the content, a Breadcrumb link is added for each level that you move through. As additional levels are added, the preceding links will compress and only their icons will display. If you mouse over one of these compressed Breadcrumb links, it will expand. If you move the mouse off of the dropdown menu, it will compress again.

Each individual Breadcrumb link has an orange down arrow . Clicking on this arrow (or anywhere in the Breadcrumb node) will open a dropdown menu that consists of all of the next level documents within the hierarchy.

The Home button will always appear at the far left of the Breadcrumb. Clicking on the button will take you to the homepage, and clicking on the orange down arrow next to icon will give you a dropdown menu of the main sections on the homepage.

ToolbarBack to top »

The toolbar is at the bottom of the site and consists of a horizontal list of tabs corresponding to sections of the site and an area beneath the list that shows icons related to those sections. Refer to the “Toolbar” section for more information.

Table of ContentsBack to top »

The table of contents is available from both the “Resources” section of the homepage and the “Tools” tab on the toolbar.

When you first open the table of contents, it shows the top level of publications. Click the “+” button next to the headings in the table of contents to expand the list and reveal subheadings and the “–” button next to the headings in the table of contents to collapse the list and hide subheadings. You may view a document by clicking the document name in the table of contents.

To synchronize the table of contents with the current document you have open, click on the “SYNC TOC” button at the top of the table of contents.

SearchBack to top »

Search is an excellent way to find the content you need. The AICPA Online Professional Library includes basic and advanced search options, the ability to refine your searches in several different ways, and a saved search function. Refer to the “Search” section for more information.

Opening a New DocumentBack to top »

To open a new document, simply click “Documents” tab in the toolbar. Buttons corresponding to documents you already have open will appear along with a “+” button. Click the “+” button. You will then have four options to start a new document (see the image below):

  • Starting at the same location as your current document
  • Performing a new search
  • Using the Quick Find tool
  • Starting at the homepage

In addition, clicking on any link in the Table of Contents (accessible from the homepage and the toolbar) will open a new document as will clicking the “new document” icon to the right of any link.

Up to 10 documents may be open at one time. If you attempt to open more than 10 documents, you will be prompted to close some of the documents you already have open.

What's New in an UpdateBack to top »

A general Online Professional Library What’s New gives a high-level view of everything that is new across all subscriptions.

In addition, each series within your subscription includes a more detailed What’s New that enables you to pinpoint exactly what has changed in an update and link directly to the updated guidance. The What’s New can serve as a starting point to find the most current guidance in your subscription.

The What’s New documents are included in a convenient list available from the Resources section of the homepage. Each What’s New is also included in the homepage pull-down list for each series.

Quick FindBack to top »

The quick find feature enables you to quickly locate a document in the AICPA Online Professional Library by clicking on the name/number of the original pronouncement. For example, if you are looking for SAS No. 115, you would select auditing standards from the document type, scroll down to SAS No. 115 in the list of standards, and follow the link. The link will take you directly to AU section 325 in AICPA Professional Standards.

Only documents included in your subscription will display in the Quick Find feature.

Application Back Button

"Next" and "Previous" ButtonsBack to top »

To move to the next screen in a document, click on the “Next” button on the right side of your screen. No “Next” button will display if you have reached the end of a document.

Once within a document, clicking this button will take you to the previous screen. The “Previous” button is located on the left side of your document screen. No “Previous” button will display if you are at the beginning of a document.

LinksBack to top »

For most links within the AICPA Online Professional Library, you have the option of opening a link in the default document or opening it in a new document. To open the link in a new document, click on the icon next to the link text.

Links from a landing page or to an external website will automatically open in a new document.

Up to 10 documents may be open at one time. If you attempt to open more than 10 documents, you will be prompted to close some of the documents you already have open.

BookmarksBack to top »

When you are within a document, you will see the Add Bookmark button in the top right of the document area. Clicking that button will add a bookmark to that section. When you’ve added a bookmark, you will notice that the button changes from “Add Bookmark” to “Delete Bookmark.”

To access your bookmarks, click the My Bookmarks button in the Tools section of the toolbar.

Here, you can delete bookmarks or go to the bookmarked section. Click on the link or the new document icon to go to your bookmarked content.

Multiple DocumentsBack to top »

The documents tab in the toolbar shows the current documents you have open. You may have up to 10 documents open at one time. Each document is represented by a tab labeled with the first few words of the title of the document. You may switch back and forth between documents by clicking the desired tab. You may also close any of the open documents by clicking the "x" button located on the right side of each document tab.

Tables and ImagesBack to top »

There are many tables and images included in the AICPA Online Professional Library. This icon appears at the top left of each table or image. If you wish to minimize the table for ease of use, click that icon. Click again to restore the table or image to full size.

Downloading a FileBack to top »

Some tools within AICPA Online Professional Library are downloadable. This gives you the ability to modify the files for your needs. These downloads are available in the Checklists and Illustrative Financial Statements, certain sections of Practice Aids, exhibits of the Management of an Accounting Practice Handbook, and the Accountant’s Business Manual. A “Download” button will display on the toolbar when this option is available.

Copying and Pasting TextBack to top »

As you view content in the AICPA Online Professional Library, you may find information that would be useful to include in a report or documentation. To save a selection of text, use your browser functions to select the section of content, copy it, and paste it into a new document.

PrintingBack to top »

You have two options for printing a document. You can click the “Print” button at the top right of your document screen or click the print icon in the “Tools” section of the toolbar.

Both of those options will bring up a preview screen:

To print the current section you are viewing, click the “Print” button. To create a PDF instead of printing, select the “print PDF” option.

When using the FASB Accounting Standards Codification™, the “Show Codification Sources” option will cause sources to be displayed in the printed document. The “Show Codification Sources” option is not available when printing a “joined” document.

FASB ASC ToolsBack to top »

AICPA Online Professional Library has several tools that are only available when working in FASB Accounting Standards Codification™. These are found within the “Tools” tab of the toolbar when you have the FASB ASC content open.

Go to
This tab provides the ability to go directly to a topic, subtopic, or section.

What Links Here
This tab provides the ability to see all FASB ASC content that links to a given paragraph in the current document.

Archive
This tab will show the archived versions of the current document.

Cross Reference
This tab presents a cross reference report that identifies where legacy material is now located.

Source
This tab causes the source to display in the document. Once selected during a session, sources will continue to display until the feature is turned off. The button will be highlighted when the sources feature is turned on. Sources will not display when sections are joined. If you do not have a FASB ASC document open, you can still use the Go To and Cross-Reference tools within both the “Tools” tab of the toolbar and the “Resources” section of the homepage.

Join Sections
This tab allows you to join all of the sections under a given topic.

A “join sections” option is also available for joining subsections. This “join sections” feature is available from the table of contents of a given section.

Adding and Editing NotesBack to top »

You can add one or more notes to content wherever you see the “Add New Note” icon . Simply click on the “Add New Note” link, type in a title and the body of your note, and click “Save.” After you’ve saved a note, you will notice a “View/Edit” link to the right of the “Add New Note” link. Clicking the “Add New Note” link again, will allow you to add another note to the same content. You can add as many notes as you wish to a content area.

You have two options for viewing and editing your notes.

  1. 1. If you click “View/Edit,” you will see the list of notes you’ve added to that content area.
  2. 2. You can also view and edit all notes that you’ve saved throughout your subscription by clicking the “Tools” button and then clicking “My Notes.” You will see the title of each of your notes, as well as the location of each note. Here you can view, edit, or delete notes. Clicking a note location will take you directly to the content area where the note is attached.

Basic SearchBack to top »

The basic search box enables you to do single or multiple word searches.

  • Enter the word(s) you want to search for and click the “Search” button.
  • The following will display:
    • A list of publications that you subscribe to and the number of search results in each title. This list may be used to refine your results to a specific title or portion of a title. For further details on this feature click here.
    • The advanced search feature, which enables you to do more precise searching. For further details on this feature click here.
    • A list of results for your search term. Click the title of the desired result to view the text of the document.
  • Once in a document, you are easily able to return to your current search results by clicking the “Search Results” button.
  • To return to the content you were viewing prior to starting your search, click the “Back” button located next to the “Search Results” button.

Advanced SearchBack to top »

The advanced search enables you to perform a focused search by selecting from a list of options. The advanced search can be reached from several places, including the “Advanced Search” button at the top of your document area, the “Resources” section of the homepage and from the “+” sign under the “Documents” tab on the toolbar.

  • Enter the word or phrase that you want to search for.
  • Select whether you want to search for "All Words," "Any Words," "Exact Phrase," or "Boolean Search".
    • All Words - Each word must be present in the document to return a hit result.
    • Any Words - At least one of the words must be present to return a hit result.
    • Exact Phrase - All words must be present in the same order that you have typed them in to return a hit result.
    • Boolean Search - Allows you to refine your queries to focus on more specific or more general information than may be found with a single word or phrase search. Click here for more details on Boolean searching.
  • You have the option to narrow your search to a specific collection of literature. To do so, select the desired collection from the dropdown menu.
  • Select whether you want to search all of the AICPA Online Professional Library or just those titles you subscribe to. To include documents not included in your current subscription, select the box for Search Unsubscribed Content.
    (Note: You will be able to click into a search result only if you subscribe to that content. A lock icon will display next to unsubscribed content and an alert will be shown containing instructions for purchasing that content.)
  • Select whether you want to show excerpts in context of the document in your search results. If you do not want excerpts to display, de-select the box for Show Excerpts.
  • Click the “Search” button to perform the search.
  • A list of results will be displayed. Click the title of the desired result to view the text of the document.

Boolean Operator SearchesBack to top »

The Boolean operators allow you to refine your queries to focus on more specific or more general information than may be found with a single word or phrase search.

Boolean operators require a word or phrase on either side of the operator. For example, dog or cat is a valid query, but or cat dog is not. The exception is the not operator. The not operator can be used before a term and still be valid. For example, not cat dog returns the same result as dog not cat. There are three Boolean operators that you can use in the AICPA Online Professional Library: and, or, not. See Operator precedence for a discussion of the precedence (order of execution) of these operators and how to override the precedence.

And
Use the And operator to search for multiple words in the same document.

The And operator may be specified in either of two ways: a single space between words or the word and.

Query Explanation
view codes
view and codes 		Finds all documents that contain both view and codes
accountant AND attorney and doctor Finds all documents that contain accountant, attorney, and doctor

Or

Use the Or operator to search for multiple words in the document collection (the words may or may not be in the same document).

The Or operator is specified using the word or.

Query Explanation
view or codes Finds all documents that contain either view or codes (may contain both)
accountant or attorney and doctor Finds all documents that contain accountant and either attorney or doctor (or both attorney and doctor) (See Operator precedence for additional explanation on these results)

Not
Use the Not operator to search for documents that do not contain a particular term or set of terms. Not is actually a unary operator (it can be used by itself), but it is often used in between two terms (find documents that contain A but not B).

Generally, the best way to think of the Not operator when used between two terms (A not B) is "find documents with A and find documents without B" (A and not B).

The Not operator is specified using the word not.

Query Explanation
view not codes Finds all documents that contain view but that do not contain codes
not account Finds all documents that do not contain account


Operator precedence
The query operators have a precedence order. That is, when two more operators are used in a query, the results of one operator will be evaluated before another operator. Understanding the precedence -- and how to override the precedence -- can assist you in finding the information you need.

The operator precedence is: Not, And, Or
If the same operators are used in a query (for example, if Or appears twice in a query), the operators are evaluated from left-to-right.

Viewing and Refining Your Search ResultsBack to top »

At the top of the Search Results page, you will see results from each library specified in your search (see “Advanced Search” for more information on searching products not in your subscription). Use these links to narrow your results to a specific library. You will be able to narrow your results even further when you drill down. As you drill down, the search results will automatically reflect the narrowing of your results.

The rest of the page shows your results by section within titles. For example, the first result in the search above is “Appendix A” in Audit Risk Alert Independence and Ethics Developments. You can click the title to go directly to that section or you can click the “+” icon next to the title to open the “Hit Details” box. This will show the subsections of that section and the number of search results within each subsection, as shown below.

Navigating Search Results Within DocumentsBack to top »

After clicking into a document that contains your search query, use the gray “Search Hits” box on the top-right of the screen to navigate through the search hits. Each hit will be highlighted in the text.

The PREV and NEXT buttons will take you to the previous and next hits within the document you are viewing. Use the ≤ and ≥ buttons in the “Search Hits” box to navigate to the previous and next documents that contain your search term. (The previous and next documents are in the same order they appeared in the search results.)

Once you’ve located your desired hit, clicking the X in the “Search Hits” box will close the box and keep your current document open.



Modifying and Saving Your SearchBack to top »

To modify your search, enter your new search words and options in the form above the search results and click the “Update Search” button. To save your search, click the “Save Search” button, then enter the desired title in the pop-up box. When you click “Submit,” the current search results will be saved.

Saved searches are retrievable from the Tools menu of the toolbar. This feature lists all of your saved searches by title and allows you to access the saved search results by clicking on a title. You can also rename or delete any of your saved searches.

Session TimeoutBack to top »

AICPA Online Professional Library will timeout after 60 minutes of idle time. Once the system times out, you will need to login again to continue working. You will receive an alert message that your session is about to timeout. If no action is taken when you receive this message, your session will automatically end about 10 minutes later. If you click OK, you will remain logged in.

Some tools within AICPA Online Professional Library contain links to videos that provide additional insight from subject matter experts. Click the video icon to open a video, which will appear in a new window.
